                         SIMPLIFIED WIRING DIAGRAM
                     Pico2 EA888.3 Car Dashboard Project
                           (last updated 2026-03-23)


  COMPONENTS
  ----------
  - Adafruit 3218    Illuminated toggle switch (latching, 3-12V, red LED)
  - Pololu 3782      D36V28F5 step-down regulator (5.3-50V in, 5V/3.2A out)
  - Adafruit 5880    I2C STEMMA QT rotary encoder (Seesaw, addr 0x36)
  - Pimoroni PIM715  Pico Display Pack 2.8" (ST7789, 320x240, SPI)
  - Raspberry Pi Pico 2 (RP2350)
  - Longan Labs 1030002  OBD-II Serial CAN Bus Dev Kit (MCP2515 + MCP2551)

  PROTECTION & FUSES
  ------------------
  - 12V rail: 3A mini blade fuse (ATM/APM) in inline holder
  - 12V rail: SS34 Schottky diode (3A, 40V, ~0.2V drop @ 90mA) — reverse polarity
  - 12V rail: SMAJ14A TVS diode (14V standoff, 400W) — transient voltage clamp
  - 5V rail:  Bourns MF-R050 PTC polyfuse (500mA hold / 1A trip, self-resetting)
  - 5V rail:  100uF electrolytic + 100nF ceramic — decoupling at Pico VSYS
  - UART:     Bidirectional level shifter if CAN kit is 5V logic (e.g. Adafruit 757)

  DATA PATH: CAN BUS
  ===================

  OBD-II Port                    Longan Labs 1030002
  +--------+                     +----------------------------+
  | Pin 6  | --- CAN-H -------> | CAN-H    (MCP2551)         |
  | Pin 14 | --- CAN-L -------> | CAN-L    (MCP2551)         |
  | Pin 16 | --- +12V --------> | VIN      (self-powered)    |
  | Pin 4/5| --- GND ---------> | GND                        |
  +--------+                     |                            |
                                 | UART TX ------+            |
                                 | UART RX ------+---+        |
                                 | GND -------> common GND   |
                                 +------------+---+-----------+
                                              |   |
                              IF 5V LOGIC:    v   v
                              +---------------------------------+
                              | Level Shifter (e.g. Adafruit    |
                              |  757, BSS138-based, 4-ch)       |
                              |                                 |
                              | HV side (5V):  kit TX, kit RX   |
                              | LV side (3.3V): GP1, GP0        |
                              | HV ref: 5V    LV ref: 3.3V     |
                              +-----------+-----+---------------+
                                          |     |
                                          v     v
                                   Pico GP1    Pico GP0
                                   (UART0 RX)  (UART0 TX)

  Default baud: 9600 (configurable).
  IMPORTANT: Verify UART voltage level before connecting directly.
  RP2350 GPIOs are 3.3V and NOT 5V-tolerant. If kit outputs 5V,
  the level shifter is mandatory. If kit supports 3.3V mode,
  connect directly and skip the shifter.


  I2C: ENCODER
  =============

  Pimoroni PIM715                JST SH 4-pin cable          Adafruit 5880
  STEMMA QT port   ---------->  (STEMMA QT / Qwiic)  -----> STEMMA QT port
  +------------+                                              +------------+
  | 3.3V       | ----------------------------------------->  | VCC (3.3V) |
  | GND        | ----------------------------------------->  | GND        |
  | GP4 (SDA)  | ----------------------------------------->  | SDA        |
  | GP5 (SCL)  | ----------------------------------------->  | SCL        |
  +------------+                                              +------------+

  Encoder has onboard 10K pull-ups. Addr 0x36 (configurable via jumpers).
  I2C0 bus at 400 kHz.  Plug-and-play, no extra wiring.

  NOTES
  =====

  1. SWITCH: The Adafruit 3218 is a latching toggle (stays ON/OFF).
     3 terminals: (+)=input, (=D)=output, (GND)=ground.
     The (=D) "headlamp" pin is both the switched output AND the
     LED anode (through an internal current-limiting resistor).
     When ON: 12V passes from (+) to (=D), LED lights, load powered.
     When OFF: circuit open, LED dark, zero standby current.
     Rated for 1-2A (sufficient; dashboard draws ~90 mA from 12V).

  2. FUSES: 3A mini blade fuse on 12V (before switch, protects wiring
     from OBD-II port). 500mA PTC polyfuse on 5V (Bourns MF-R050,
     500mA hold / 1A trip; self-resetting after fault clears).
     Actual draw: ~150 mA on 5V, ~90 mA on 12V.

  3. REVERSE POLARITY: SS34 Schottky diode in series on 12V input
     (after fuse, before switch). Blocks reverse-wired 12V from
     reaching any downstream component. ~0.2V forward drop at
     90 mA is negligible (0.5V max at 3A). Cheap insurance against
     wiring mistakes.

  4. TVS CLAMP: SMAJ14A transient voltage suppressor across the 12V
     rail to GND (after fuse, before switch). Automotive 12V is
     noisy — load dumps can spike to 40-80V, alternator transients,
     relay inductive kick. The TVS clamps to ~23V peak, protecting
     the Pololu (rated 50V) and the switch. Cathode band toward +12V.

  5. DECOUPLING: 100uF electrolytic + 100nF ceramic at Pico VSYS pin.
     Smooths voltage dips from PTC resistance and long wire runs
     between the Pololu output and the Pico. Place physically close
     to the VSYS pin.

  6. POLOLU EN PIN: Left floating = enabled (internal 100k pull-up to
     VIN). To add a soft-shutdown from firmware, connect EN to a GPIO
     and drive low to enter <20uA/V sleep mode.

  7. CAN KIT VOLTAGE: The Longan Labs 1030002 powers itself from
     OBD-II 12V. Its UART logic level may be 5V. RP2350 GPIOs are
     3.3V and NOT 5V-tolerant. Use a bidirectional level shifter
     (e.g. Adafruit 757, BSS138-based) on GP0/GP1 if kit is 5V.
     If kit supports 3.3V mode, connect directly.

  8. CAN KIT POWER: The kit is always powered from OBD-II pin 16
     (constant 12V) and does NOT go through the Adafruit 3218 switch.
     It stays on when the dashboard switch is off. To power it down
     with the switch, splice the kit's 12V through the switch output.

  9. ENCODER: Connects to PIM715's STEMMA QT port with a standard
     JST SH 4-pin cable (included with encoder). No soldering.
     Powered at 3.3V from the PIM715. I2C address 0x36 (default).
     Up to 8 encoders can be daisy-chained via address jumpers.

  10. PIM715 RGB LED: GP6/GP7/GP8 are physically wired to the RGB LED
      on the display pack. These pins are NOT free for other use even
      if the firmware doesn't drive them.

  11. NOT NEEDED: Flyback diode (no inductive loads), ESD on I2C
      (internal connector, not user-facing), fuse after switch
      (blade fuse before switch already covers the full path).
